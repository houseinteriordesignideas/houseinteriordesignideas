1st Grade Definition of Main Idea

The main idea is the most important part of a story. It is what the story is all about. The main idea can be found in the beginning, middle, or end of a story.

How to Find the Main Idea

There are a few ways to find the main idea of a story.

  • Look for the topic sentence. The topic sentence is the sentence that tells you what the story is about. It is usually found in the beginning of the story.
  • Identify the key details. The key details are the most important facts in the story. They support the main idea.
  • Summarize the story. A summary is a short retelling of the story. It includes the main idea and the key details.

Example

Here is an example of a story with a main idea.

The Lion and the Mouse

Once upon a time, there was a lion who was sleeping in the forest. A mouse ran over the lion’s paw and woke him up. The lion was angry and was about to eat the mouse. The mouse begged the lion to spare his life. The lion laughed and said, "Why should I spare your life? You are so small and weak."

The mouse said, "One day, I may be able to help you." The lion thought this was funny, but he let the mouse go.

A few days later, the lion was caught in a hunter’s trap. He roared and tried to get free, but he couldn’t. The mouse heard the lion’s roar and ran to help. The mouse chewed on the ropes of the trap until they broke. The lion was free!

The lion was grateful to the mouse for saving his life. He realized that even the smallest creature can be helpful.

Main Idea: The main idea of this story is that even the smallest creature can be helpful.
